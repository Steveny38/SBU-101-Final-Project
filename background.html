    • Background Info

    Domestic Violence is any form of abusive behavior occurring within family members, ex-spouses, intimate partners, former intimate partners, couples, and former dating couples. In these situations, one party aims to establish or maintain power and control over the other partner. The couple or former couple involved can be of the same or opposite sex. This type of violence manifests in various ways, including physical, sexual, emotional, and psychological actions, as well as threats of such actions, with the intention of creating influence over another person. Exemplifying behaviors that are aimed to intimidate, manipulate, humiliate, isolate, frighten, terrorize, coerce, threaten, blame, hurt, injure, or wound someone.

    There are 2 Categories of Domestic Violence

    Intimate Partner Violence

    Domestic violence encompasses "Intimate Partner Violence," involving both present and past spouses, as well as current and former dating couples, whether or not they share a child. This form of violence is not limited to heterosexual or same-sex couples and doesn't necessarily involve sexual intimacy. The frequency and severity of Intimate Partner Violence can vary, from isolated incidents with potential impact to persistent and severe battering. Over 1 in 3 women (35.6%) and 1 in 4 men (28.5%) in the US have experienced intimate partner violence in their lifetime.

    Non-Intimate Partner Violence

    Domestic Violence also extends to "Non-Intimate Partner Violence," referring to violence between individuals who are not intimate partners but share a familial relationship, such as a parent, an adult, son/daughter, or siblings.
